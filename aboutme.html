About Me

Hi, my name is Josh McFarlin, and I am a first year Computer Science major at the Georgia Institute of Technology in Atlanta, Georgia.

I have five years of programming experience, primarily focusing in Python, but I have been expanding my skills into Java and also mobile and web languages. My primary work setup includes PyCharm, IntelliJ, and WebStorm for programming, and Git and Github for version control. Expanding on my Python skill is experience with the Django and Flask frameworks to create web apps and APIs. I also have experience with Ionic framework, as I have created two cross-platform apps. The many projects I have worked on have also resulted in experience with different forms of databases such as SQLite, MySQL, and Firebase.

My true passion in Computer Science lies in blockchain technology, and I am currently focusing on becoming a blockchain engineer. Blockchain technology is opening new channels for everyday people and computers to interact with data and each other like never before, and I find it an incredibly interesting and exciting field.

While working on front end designs, I focus on creating functional, smooth, and beautiful user interfaces to create programs that are simple and fluid. I'm a big fan of flat design and minimalism, and I try to create interfaces that reflect these principles.

I am actively seeking internships and projects to work on, if you are interested by my work, feel free to contact me.
